================================================================================
TOPIC 1: Early Medieval North India (c. 700–1200)
================================================================================
Folder: Early_Medieval_North_India

Subtopics (detailed):
- Sources: inscriptions (e.g. Gwalior, Hathigumpha), texts (Rajatarangini, Prithviraj Raso), foreign accounts.
- Tripartite struggle: Palas (Bengal-Bihar), Pratiharas (North/Central India), Rashtrakutas (Deccan); struggle for Kannauj.
- Pala dynasty: Gopala, Dharmapala, Devapala; Buddhism, Nalanda, Vikramashila.
- Pratihara dynasty: Nagabhata, Bhoja I, Mihira Bhoja; defence against Arabs.
- Rashtrakutas: Dantidurga, Krishna I (Kailash, Ellora), Amoghavarsha; Kannada literature.
- Rajput dynasties: Chahamanas/Chauhans (Ajmer, Prithviraj III), Paramaras (Malwa, Bhoja), Chandelas (Khajuraho), Gahadavalas (Kannauj), Kalachuris, Tomaras (Delhi).
- Arab conquest of Sind: Muhammad bin Qasim (712); Al-Hind.
- Mahmud of Ghazni: raids (1000–1026), Somnath, wealth and destruction.
- Muhammad Ghori: First and Second Battle of Tarain (1191, 1192), defeat of Prithviraj; Qutbuddin Aibak as commander.
- Society and economy: feudalism debate, land grants, trade, temples.

Files:
1	Tripartite struggle, Palas, Pratiharas, Rashtrakutas
2	Rajput dynasties (Chauhans, Paramaras, Chandelas, etc.)
3	Arab and Turkic invasions (Sind, Mahmud, Ghori), Tarain

================================================================================
TOPIC 2: Delhi Sultanate – Slave and Khalji Dynasties (1206–1320)
================================================================================
Folder: Delhi_Sultanate_Slave_Khalji

Subtopics:
- Slave dynasty: Qutbuddin Aibak (1206–1210), construction of Qutb Minar; Iltutmish (1211–1236), consolidation, iqta; Razia Sultan (1236–1240); Balban (1266–1286), theory of kingship, blood and iron.
- Khalji dynasty: Jalaluddin Khalji (1290–1296); Alauddin Khalji (1296–1316)—market reforms (control of prices, Diwan-i-Riyasat), Mongol raids, conquests (Gujarat, Ranthambore, Chittor, South India), Malik Kafur.
- Administration: Sultan, wazir, iqta, army, revenue.
- Architecture: Qutb complex, Alai Darwaza, early Indo-Islamic.

Files:
1	Slave dynasty (Aibak, Iltutmish, Razia, Balban)
2	Alauddin Khalji (reforms, conquests, administration)

================================================================================
TOPIC 3: Delhi Sultanate – Tughlaq, Sayyid, Lodi (1320–1526)
================================================================================
Folder: Delhi_Sultanate_Tughlaq_Sayyid_Lodi

Subtopics:
- Tughlaq: Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq (1320–1325); Muhammad bin Tughlaq (1325–1351)—token currency, transfer of capital to Daulatabad, experiments, Barani’s account; Firoz Shah Tughlaq (1351–1388)—reforms, canals, slavery.
- Timur’s invasion (1398), sack of Delhi.
- Sayyid dynasty (1414–1451): Khizr Khan.
- Lodi dynasty (1451–1526): Bahlul Lodi, Sikandar Lodi, Ibrahim Lodi; First Battle of Panipat (1526) with Babur.
- Decline: provincial independence, rise of regional kingdoms.

Files:
1	Muhammad bin Tughlaq and Firoz Shah Tughlaq
2	Timur, Sayyid, Lodi; Battle of Panipat 1526

================================================================================
TOPIC 4: Sultanate Administration, Society and Culture
================================================================================
Folder: Sultanate_Administration_Society

Subtopics:
- Administration: Sultan, nobility (Turks, Persians, Indian Muslims), iqta and iqtadar, revenue (kharaj, jizya), army (bandagan, ariz).
- Society: Hindus under Sultanate, ulema, sufis, slaves, caste and conversion.
- Economy: land revenue, trade (Delhi, ports), coinage.
- Culture: Persian language, history writing (Barani, Amir Khusrau), architecture (slave and Khalji phase), music (Amir Khusrau).

Files:
1	Iqta, revenue, army; society and economy
2	Culture: Persian, Amir Khusrau, architecture

================================================================================
TOPIC 5: Vijayanagara and Bahmani Kingdoms (Deccan)
================================================================================
Folder: Vijayanagara_Bahmani

Subtopics:
- Vijayanagara Empire: founding (Harihara and Bukka, 1336), Sangama, Saluva, Tuluva dynasties; Krishna Deva Raya (1509–1529), expansion, patronage; Battle of Talikota (1565), decline.
- Administration: nayankara, amaram, revenue; capital Hampi.
- Bahmani Sultanate (1347–1527): Alauddin Bahman Shah, Mahmud Gawan (minister); disintegration into five Deccan Sultanates (Bijapur, Golkonda, Ahmadnagar, Berar, Bidar).
- Culture: Vitthala temple, Hazara Rama, literature (Telugu, Kannada, Sanskrit).

Files:
1	Vijayanagara (founding, Krishna Deva Raya, Talikota)
2	Bahmani and Deccan Sultanates; administration and culture

================================================================================
TOPIC 6: Bhakti and Sufi Movements
================================================================================
Folder: Bhakti_Sufi

Subtopics:
- Bhakti in South India: Alvars and Nayanars (Tamil), Ramanuja (Vishishtadvaita), Basava (Virashaiva).
- Bhakti in North India: Ramananda, Kabir, Guru Nanak (Sikhism), Chaitanya (Bengal), Mira Bai, Tulsidas (Ramcharitmanas), Surdas; Nirguna and Saguna bhakti.
- Sufism: Chishti (Moinuddin Chishti, Nizamuddin Auliya), Suhrawardi, Qadiri; khanqah, silsila; impact on society and culture.
- Interaction: Hindu-Muslim syncretism, criticism of ritual, vernacular literature.

Files:
1	Bhakti (South: Alvars, Nayanars, Ramanuja; North: Kabir, Nanak, Mira, Tulsidas)
2	Sufi orders (Chishti, Suhrawardi); impact and syncretism

================================================================================
TOPIC 7: Mughal Empire – Early (Babur, Humayun, Sher Shah)
================================================================================
Folder: Mughal_Early

Subtopics:
- Babur (1526–1530): First Battle of Panipat (1526) vs Ibrahim Lodi, Battle of Khanwa (1527) vs Rana Sanga; Tuzuk-i-Baburi (Baburnama).
- Humayun (1530–1540, 1555–1556): defeat by Sher Shah, exile, reconquest.
- Sher Shah Suri (1540–1545): Sur Empire, administration (sarkar, pargana, land revenue), Grand Trunk Road, currency (rupaya), postal system.
- Humayun’s return and early Mughal consolidation.

Files:
1	Babur (Panipat, Khanwa, Baburnama)
2	Humayun, Sher Shah Suri (administration, Grand Trunk Road)

================================================================================
TOPIC 8: Mughal Empire – Akbar to Aurangzeb
================================================================================
Folder: Mughal_Akbar_To_Aurangzeb

Subtopics:
- Akbar (1556–1605): expansion (Gujarat, Bengal, Kashmir, Deccan), Rajput policy (marriage, mansab), Din-i-Ilahi, Ibadat Khana, Navratnas (Birbal, Tansen, Abul Fazl), Ain-i-Akbari; land revenue (Todar Mal, zabt).
- Jahangir (1605–1627): Nur Jahan, Mughal painting, chain of justice.
- Shah Jahan (1628–1658): Taj Mahal, Red Fort (Delhi), Jama Masjid, Peacock Throne; war of succession.
- Aurangzeb (1658–1707): expansion in Deccan, conflict with Marathas, religious policies (jizya reimposed), decline.

Files:
1	Akbar (expansion, Rajput policy, Din-i-Ilahi, administration)
2	Jahangir, Shah Jahan (Taj, Red Fort), Aurangzeb (Deccan, policies)

Files:

================================================================================
TOPIC 10: Marathas and Later Mughals
================================================================================
Folder: Marathas_Later_Mughals

Subtopics:
- Shivaji (1627–1680): rise, administration (Ashta Pradhan), revenue (chauth, sardeshmukhi), conflict with Bijapur and Mughals; coronation (1674).
- Maratha Confederacy: Peshwas (Balaji Vishwanath, Baji Rao I), expansion; Third Battle of Panipat (1761) vs Ahmad Shah Abdali.
- Later Mughals: Bahadur Shah I to Bahadur Shah Zafar; Nadir Shah’s invasion (1739), Abdali’s raids.
- Rise of regional states: Awadh, Bengal, Hyderabad, Mysore (Haider, Tipu), Sikhs, Jats.

Files:
1	Shivaji (administration, Ashta Pradhan, chauth)
2	Peshwas, Panipat 1761; later Mughals, Nadir Shah, regional states
