TOPIC 1: British Expansion and Conquest (c. 1757–1856)
================================================================================
Folder: British_Expansion_Conquest

Subtopics (detailed):
- Bengal: Battle of Plassey (1757, Clive vs Siraj-ud-Daulah), Battle of Buxar (1764), Dual Government (1765–1772), Diwani of Bengal, Bihar, Orissa; Robert Clive, Warren Hastings.
- Mysore: Anglo-Mysore Wars (I–IV), Haider Ali, Tipu Sultan, Treaty of Srirangapatnam (1792), Fall of Srirangapatnam (1799).
- Marathas: Anglo-Maratha Wars (I–III), Treaty of Salbai (1782), Treaty of Bassein (1802), Peshwa Baji Rao II; Scindia, Holkar, Bhonsle; end of Peshwa (1818).
- Punjab and Sind: Ranjit Singh, Anglo-Sikh Wars (1845–1846, 1848–1849), annexation of Punjab (1849); annexation of Sind (1843, Charles Napier).
- Other: Subsidiary Alliance (Wellesley), Doctrine of Lapse (Dalhousie), annexation of Awadh (1856), Satara, Nagpur, Jhansi.
- Key figures: Clive, Hastings, Cornwallis, Wellesley, Dalhousie, Bentinck.

Files:
1	Bengal (Plassey, Buxar, Dual Government, Diwani); Clive, Hastings
2	Mysore (Haider, Tipu); Marathas (Anglo-Maratha Wars, Peshwa)
3	Punjab, Sind; Subsidiary Alliance, Doctrine of Lapse; Dalhousie

TOPIC 2: British Administration and Economy (c. 1773–1858)
================================================================================
Folder: British_Administration_Economy

Subtopics:
- Constitutional: Regulating Act (1773), Pitt’s India Act (1784), Charter Acts (1793, 1813, 1833, 1853); Governor-General, Council, Supreme Court.
- Revenue: Permanent Settlement (Bengal, 1793—Cornwallis, zamindari), Ryotwari (Madras, Bombay—Munro, Thomas Munro), Mahalwari (North India—Holt Mackenzie); land revenue systems and impact.
- Civil services: Covenanted vs uncovenanted; Indian Civil Service (ICS), open competition (1853).
- Army and police: British vs Indian troops; reorganization after 1857.
- Education: Orientalist vs Anglicist; Macaulay’s Minute (1835), English education; Wood’s Despatch (1854).
- Economy: Drain of wealth (Dadabhai Naoroji), deindustrialization, commercialization of agriculture, railways (Dalhousie), telegraph, post.
- Social: Abolition of Sati (1829), Widow Remarriage Act (1856), suppression of thugi.

Files:
1	Regulating Act, Pitt’s India Act, Charter Acts; Governor-General
2	Revenue: Permanent Settlement, Ryotwari, Mahalwari
3	Education (Macaulay, Wood’s Despatch); economy (drain, railways); social reforms

TOPIC 3: Revolt of 1857
================================================================================
Folder: Revolt_1857

Subtopics:
- Causes: Political (Doctrine of Lapse, annexations), economic (revenue, land), military (greased cartridges, service conditions), social-religious (interference).
- Outbreak: Meerut (May 1857), Delhi (Bahadur Shah Zafar II as nominal head), Kanpur (Nana Saheb, Tantia Tope), Lucknow (Begum Hazrat Mahal), Jhansi (Rani Lakshmibai), Central India.
- Leaders: Bahadur Shah Zafar II, Nana Saheb, Rani Lakshmibai, Tantia Tope, Kunwar Singh, Begum Hazrat Mahal.
- Suppression: British reconquest of Delhi, Lucknow, Jhansi; role of loyal princes and troops.
- Consequences: End of Company rule, Government of India Act 1858, Queen’s Proclamation (1858), end of Mughal dynasty; army reorganization; policy changes.

Files:
1	Causes, outbreak (Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur, Lucknow, Jhansi)
2	Leaders; suppression; consequences (1858 Act, Queen’s Proclamation)

TOPIC 4: Socio-Religious Reform Movements (c. 1820–1900)
================================================================================
Folder: Socio_Religious_Reforms

Subtopics:
- Bengal: Raja Rammohan Roy, Brahmo Samaj (1828), Brahmo Samaj of India (Keshub Chandra Sen), Sadharan Brahmo Samaj; Young Bengal (Derozio); Ramakrishna and Vivekananda (Ramakrishna Mission, 1897).
- Maharashtra: Prarthana Samaj (1867—Ranade, Bhandarkar), Paramahansa Mandali; Jyotiba Phule (Satyashodhak Samaj, 1873), women’s education.
- North India: Arya Samaj (1875—Dayanand Saraswati), Shuddhi, Gurukuls; Sir Syed Ahmad Khan (Aligarh Movement, MAO College 1875, Aligarh Muslim University).
- Other: Theosophical Society (Blavatsky, Olcott; Annie Besant); Sikh reform (Singh Sabha); Parsi reform.
- Themes: Caste, women (sati, widow remarriage, education), education, vernacular press, rationalism vs tradition.

Files:
1	Raja Rammohan Roy, Brahmo Samaj; Young Bengal; Ramakrishna, Vivekananda
2	Prarthana Samaj; Jyotiba Phule; Arya Samaj; Sir Syed, Aligarh; Theosophical

TOPIC 5: Rise of Nationalism and Early Congress (c. 1885–1905)
================================================================================
Folder: Rise_Of_Nationalism_Early_Congress

Subtopics:
- Pre-Congress: Associations (Landholders’ Society, British India Association), provincial associations; Ilbert Bill (1883) controversy; Vernacular Press Act (1878).
- Foundation of INC: Bombay (1885), A.O. Hume, W.C. Bonnerjee, first session; early aims (loyal, constitutional).
- Moderates (1885–1905): Dadabhai Naoroji (drain of wealth, poverty and un-British rule), Gopal Krishna Gokhale, Surendranath Banerjea, Pherozeshah Mehta; methods (petitions, resolutions); economic critique.
- Extremists: Emergence of Lal-Bal-Pal (Lala Lajpat Rai, Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Bipin Chandra Pal); Tilak (Ganesh Chaturthi, Shivaji festivals, Kesari); Swadeshi and Boycott (post-1905).
- Partition of Bengal (1905): Curzon, anti-partition agitation, Swadeshi movement, Boycott; annulment (1911).

Files:
1	Pre-Congress associations; foundation of INC (1885); Moderates
2	Extremists (Lal-Bal-Pal, Tilak); Partition of Bengal (1905), Swadeshi, Boycott

TOPIC 6: Gandhian Phase and Mass Movements (c. 1915–1947)
================================================================================
Folder: Gandhian_Phase_Mass_Movements

Subtopics:
- Gandhi’s entry: South Africa (satyagraha), return to India (1915); Champaran (1917), Kheda (1918), Ahmedabad Mill (1918).
- Rowlatt Act (1919), Jallianwala Bagh (1919, General Dyer), Hunter Commission.
- Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–1922): Khilafat (Ali brothers), Gandhi’s leadership; suspension after Chauri Chaura (1922).
- Civil Disobedience (1930–1934): Salt Satyagraha (Dandi March 1930), Gandhi-Irwin Pact (1931), Round Table Conferences; Poona Pact (1932, Ambedkar—reserved seats).
- Quit India Movement (1942): “Do or Die”; repression; INA and Subhas Chandra Bose (separate topic overlap).
- Key themes: Satyagraha, non-violence, constructive program (khadi, Harijan, education), women’s participation.

Files:
1	Gandhi’s early satyagrahas; Rowlatt, Jallianwala Bagh; Non-Cooperation, Khilafat
2	Civil Disobedience (Salt March, Gandhi-Irwin, RTC); Poona Pact
3	Quit India (1942); constructive program; role of women

================================================================================
Folder: Constitutional_Development_Communal

Subtopics:
- Reforms: Morley-Minto (1909)—separate electorates for Muslims; Montagu-Chelmsford (1919)—dyarchy, provincial councils; Government of India Act 1935—provincial autonomy, federation (not implemented), Sind, Orissa.
- Communal: Muslim League (1906), Simla Deputation (1906); Lucknow Pact (1916); growth of communalism; Jinnah, Two-Nation Theory; Lahore Resolution (1940—“Pakistan”).
- Congress and League: Negotiations (Cripps 1942, Wavell Plan, Cabinet Mission 1946); Direct Action Day (1946); Mountbatten Plan (1947), Partition, Independence (15 August 1947).

1	Morley-Minto, Montagu-Chelmsford, Government of India Act 1935
